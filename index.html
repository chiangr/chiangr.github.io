Hi! I’m Ryan Chiang, currently a third-year student at UC Berkeley. I’m actively searching for internship positions for Summer/Fall 2024.


Originally from Walnut, CA, I spend my free time pursuing various hobbies. I enjoy golfing, boxing, and playing soccer. Additionally, I love tinkering with my 3D printers and electronics station, where I engage in hands-on learning and create exciting projects. There’s something incredibly satisfying about holding the end product in my own hands. My journey through the mechanical engineering department has ignited my passion for understanding how the world works, particularly through the lens of mechanics and hardware.


During my grade school years, I dabbled in web design, which led me to continue exploring UI/UX design. As a web developer for SAAS Berkeley, I’ve honed my creative ideation skills and learned to effectively communicate user experiences in products and projects. While I don’t plan to pursue frontend development as a career, this experience has been invaluable in bridging the gap between software engineering and hardware/mechanics. I now apply my knowledge of Python, MATLAB, and C/C++ to personal projects and research.
